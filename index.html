Hi I'm Matt. I'm a media professional in New York City. I am also a burgeoning coder.

I earned my bachelor's from the Missouri School of Journalism, where I emphasized on multimedia storytelling. While in college, I contributed work to local news organizations, like the Columbia Missourian and KBIA (mid-Missouri's NPR-member station). My work has also appeared in The Hartford Courant. As a graphics reporter at the Columbia Missourian, I used digital tools like Highcharts.js and D3.js to create news-oriented data visualizations. I also focused on innovation in journalism at the Reynolds Journalism Institute where I studied and worked on new methods of storytelling, production and distribution.

After graduating I started working at ThePointsGuy.com, where I was the assistant editor. In this role, I wrote news stories, managed social media and publishing, worked on analytics, and much more. Later on I resigned from position with intentions of law school, but ultimately decided not to attend, even after months of applying, LSATs and acceptances. I traveled for a while, and completed the Camino de Santiago in Spain, where I hiked 35 days and more than 500 miles from the Spanish border at Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port, France to Spain's Atlantic Coast in Fisterra, Spain.

Now – back in New York City – I am applying to coding bootcamps with aspirations to be a professional coder.

Outside of work, I enjoy running, podcasts, reading and cooking.
